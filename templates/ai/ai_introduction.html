Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science focused on creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding. AI aims to develop machines that can simulate human cognitive functions to solve complex problems, adapt to new situations, and make decisions.

Key Points

  • Definition:AI involves creating intelligent agents or systems that can mimic human abilities.
  • Goal:To develop machines that can perform tasks requiring human-like thinking.
  • Applications:Includes robotics, natural language processing, computer vision, and expert systems.

Types of A.I.

  1. 1>.Narrow A.I.

    6. Definition: AI systems designed and trained to handle a specific task or a narrow range of tasks.
    Capabilities: Operates under predefined rules and cannot perform beyond its programmed limits.
    Examples: Virtual assistants (e.g., Siri, Alexa), recommendation systems (e.g., Netflix suggestions), and image recognition software.
    Scope: Limited to particular applications with no general understanding or awareness.
    Development: Currently the most common type of AI, used in many everyday applications.

  6. 2>.General A.I.(AGI - Artificial General Intelligence)

    11. Definition: AI systems with general cognitive abilities that can understand, learn, and apply knowledge across a broad range of tasks, similar to human intelligence.
    Capabilities: Can perform any intellectual task that a human can, including reasoning, problem-solving, and understanding complex concepts.
    Examples: No existing systems yet; theoretical in nature.
    Scope: Broad and adaptable, with the ability to transfer knowledge across different domains.
    Development: Still under research, with no practical implementations as of now.

  11. 3>.Strong A.I.(Full A.I.)

    16. Definition: AI systems with consciousness, self-awareness, and understanding akin to human beings.
    Capabilities: Can exhibit human-like understanding, reasoning, and emotional responses.
    Examples: Hypothetical and not yet realized; often featured in science fiction.
    Scope: Includes not only solving tasks but also possessing a level of self-awareness and subjective experience.
    Development: An advanced concept still debated among researchers and philosophers, with no current technological realization.
